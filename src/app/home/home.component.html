A Little About Me


I managed an IT Department for over 15 years. Our company grew from a few offices to over twenty offices around Indiana while I was there. During that time I learned many facets about IT from building custom desktops to managing Active Directory, Exchange and DNS servers to creating VPNs between offices using various routers. Through out this time I used things like batch files, Powershell, Winforms and .NET libraries to help automate common tasks such as user onboarding, reminder emails and converting paper forms to automated electronic versions.

The things that I enjoyed the most in the IT Department were the things that had to do with programming. When I decided on a career change, I wanted to target solving problems with programming. So, after researching the best bootcamps around, I moved out to Colorado and enrolled in a 6 month long emersive web development program at Galvanize to refresh my skills for the current landscape.

I was immediately drawn to the graphics capabilities and that passion continues to this day. Whenever I'm learning a new language, one of the first things I ask myself is; I wonder how I can draw things?
